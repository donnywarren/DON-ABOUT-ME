     That's me, but please call me Don. My mom used to call me Curious George. I always want to know more about everything. There never seems to be a limit to the things I want to learn. Currently, I am very excited to dive into the ocean of knowledge available in computer science and programming.

    I'm transitioning from retail/restaurants to tech because I want new challenges and new experiences. I enjoyed the comradery and fast pace of the restaurant industry. I'm looking forward to applying my nimble and adaptive work style to the ever changing tech industry. I'm a "we can fix anything" type of person, so I believe I'm going to have a positive impact in all my future rolls.

    I'm also curious to learn if my corny sense of humor and chatty personality will continue to help me make friends and develop strong work relationships when I'm a software engineer. Moving to New York meant saying good-by to many friends and coworkers, but fortunately I've been able to continue mentoring and coaching from a distance. I've always been a high achiever and always was considered the "friendliest boss I've ever had" by my teams. The dichotomy of my personality was spotted early when my teachers complimented my top-of-the-class work and then encouraged less talk and more focus.

    Moving to New York and starting over has been the best decision of my life. I have always known where I wanted to live and I can't believe I waited so long to get here. My ability to solve problems and achieve complex goals has made the transition a snap. I love the subway, and also architecture, science fiction and dessert. Maybe I will work on a team that designs ice cream parlors on Mars?

    Currently I am without social or financial obligations, therefore it's an excellent time for me to devote myself to learning software engineering. I don't know which business or specific job title I want to land. I do know I want to connect new services to new users by bridging the gap between technical realities and user goals. Thanks!
